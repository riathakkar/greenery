Landfill

  • Once your throw away your trash it will either end u p in a landfill a recycling center, or inside an incinerator where trash is bruned ans turned into energy.
  • If waste is not correctly managed it can end up in the ocean or on the streets
  • Each person produces on average 4.4 lbs. of waste
  • Gases created in landfills can produce toxic gases which impact the air quality in that area
  • Objects that include chemicals, such as household cleaners, mix over time and can cause water pollution
  • Americans generate 254 million tons of trash every year

Recycle

  • Recyclables are sorted at a Materials Recovery Facility
  • Lauren Singer, a New Yorker, fit two years of trash into a single mason jar!
  • The EPA estimates that 75% of the American waste stream is recyclable, but we only recycle about 30% of it.
  • Americans throw away about 21.5 million pounds of food a year. If this food was composted it would reduce the same amount of greenhouse gas as taking 2 MILLION cars of the road
  • A glass container can go from a recycling bin to a store shelf in as few as 30 days.
  • Approximately 1 billion trees worth of paper are thrown away every year in the U.S.
  • Every year we throw out enough glass jars and bottles to fill an entire skyscraper, even though they are recyclable!

Compost

  • Once compostable items are thrown away they are taken to something called a receiving building
  • 200 trucks a day come in and out of the facility
  • 80% of compost is yard waste and 20% is food waste
  • Once compost is unloaded separation process begins, where they follow something called a recipe: Carbon ( brown wood chips ),Nitrogen (green organics ), Inoculate (aged compost )
